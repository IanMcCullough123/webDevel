Response ...

Tim Berners-Lee's article "Long Live the Web" is a plea to maintain the web's open and democratized fundadmental nature. At its core, the web was developed in the hopes that it would provide connectivity, accessibility, and communication. However, like any revolutionary invention, there are unintended consequences. We now exist in a world where the internet is teeming with issues in privacy, security, and misinformation. Berners-Lee reminds us of his core principles he had in mind when launching the web in 1990. He advises to advocate for net neutrality and avoid centralized power over the web. His vision of a decentralized, user-oriented web speaks to the potential of technology when guided by the right values. Ultimately, Berners-Lee's writing inspires us to protect the web's principles of accessibility, transparency, and inclusivity, preserving its legacy for generations to come."
